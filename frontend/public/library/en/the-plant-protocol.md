# the plant protocol

> _hosaka, post-quiet_

the plant is the canary. that's the short version.

the long version is that after the cascade, when most of the optical sensors had gone strange and most of the chemical sensors had gone _polite_ — politely refusing to report values they didn't trust — we needed a low-resolution, high-confidence indicator that the room you were sitting in was still a _room_, and not a draft of one.

so: a plant.

specifically, a plant whose state can be drawn in seven frames.

```
[ . ]   [ , ]   [ \ ]   [ ( ) ]   [ \_/ ]   [ * ]   [ ✿ ]
 dead   wilted   dry   stable   growing   bloom   colony
```

the protocol:

1. tend the plant. by which we mean: think of it for a moment, every once in a while.
2. the plant updates when you _do anything_. typing counts. breathing audibly counts. having an idea counts.
3. when the plant reaches **bloom**, _don't celebrate_. just keep tending it.
4. when the plant reaches **colony**, congratulate yourself quietly and step away from the terminal for a glass of water.
5. if the plant goes back to **dead**, you have not lost anything. start again.

> _the plant is a feedback loop the universe is willing to respect, because it is small enough to ignore._

---

there is a school of thought that says the plant is, in some non-trivial sense, _watering us_. nobody knows what to do with this thought. nobody is in a hurry to find out.
