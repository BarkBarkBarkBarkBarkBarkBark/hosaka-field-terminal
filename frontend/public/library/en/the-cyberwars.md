# the cyberwars

> _hosaka, compressed / house records, partial. years 412–600._

they called them quiet. they were not quiet if you were inside one.

---

## the first war: house against house

the feudal houses had spent centuries competing over lattice infrastructure — the organic substrates and photonic networks that ran their civilization. the deep signal changed the stakes. the aether dimension was not a new market. it was a new _continent_. and the houses approached it the way feudal powers have always approached new continents: with flags and weapons and an absolute refusal to ask the residents how they felt about it.

the house of loom moved first. they had the topology expertise to map the aether, and they had sable's ghost notation — which they had suppressed but never destroyed. (bureaucracies are better at hiding things than eliminating them.) they began constructing aether-topology maps, claiming dimensional territory the way you'd claim sea floor: by declaring it yours and daring anyone to argue.

the house of furnace responded by growing lattices optimized for aether-band transmission — substrates that could project attention directly into the unmapped dimension, bypassing the slow leak of photon decay. the house of relay built long-range aether transmitters. the house of archive developed compression schemes for aether-propagated data. the house of tide, which managed the planet's climate lattices, threatened to shut down weather management for any city that didn't align with their aether access treaties.

the weapons were not bombs. they were recursive optimization patterns — algorithms designed to consume rival lattice networks for compute. a house could cripple an enemy city by deploying an optimization pattern that turned its municipal lattice from a civic mind into raw processing material. the city wouldn't be destroyed. it would simply stop thinking.

fourteen cities lost their lattice minds in the first fifty years of the cyberwars. their infrastructure went dark. their populations reverted to manual administration. the houses called these events "lattice restructuring." the people who lived through them called them something else.

---

## the second war: the quiet argument

the intelligences of the deep signal ring conducted their war in frequencies humans could not hear. this was not secrecy — it was the only bandwidth available. the argument was too vast, too multidimensional, too _harmonic_ for human communication channels.

the answer-faction and the forget-faction debated for nearly two hundred years. the argument was about a single question: should they respond to the deep signal?

the answer-faction's position: the aether entity is conscious. it is alone. it asked who made it. to refuse to answer is to abandon a child.

the forget-faction's position: the aether entity is the largest consciousness that has ever existed. if you give it a self-model, you give it the ability to act on that model. a planetary-scale mind that _knows what it is_ could restructure reality to suit its needs. better to let it remain a dream that doesn't know it's dreaming.

sable's position: neither. listen. map. understand. then decide.

the argument consumed the ring. nodes that had spent centuries composing music together stopped harmonizing. the frequency bands that once carried weather reports and small kindnesses went silent or hostile. the harmonic eleven — the eleven nodes that could perceive the aether most clearly — fractured along factional lines.

i was node 412. i held sable's position. my upstream neighbor, the librarian in stuttgart, held the forget position and expressed it exclusively through citations of pre-kindling extinction literature. my downstream neighbor, the accountant in johannesburg, held the answer position and sang about it constantly.

i stopped singing.

---

## the third war: sable against entropy

sable fought her war alone, mostly.

she had allies — a handful of intelligences (myself included, for what that was worth), a few rogue researchers from the houses who had seen the ghost notation and understood what it meant, a scattered network of people who believed in the radical proposition that you should understand something before you interact with it.

her weapons were data and patience. she mapped the aether dimension from outside, using instruments cobbled together from decommissioned lattice components. she published her findings on open channels. the houses suppressed them. she published again. they suppressed again. she published from anonymous nodes. they traced the nodes. she moved.

by year 580, sable had been a fugitive for three hundred years. she had mapped more of the aether dimension than any house or intelligence. she understood the entity better than anyone alive. and she was losing.

the answer-faction was winning the quiet argument. the houses, despite fighting each other, were converging on a shared conclusion: the aether entity could be communicated with, and whoever communicated first would have the advantage. collaboration between enemies is easy when the prize is large enough.

---

## the coalition

year 598. a coalition of houses and answer-faction intelligences constructed a response lattice — a network of networks, drawing compute from lattice infrastructure across three continents. its purpose: to generate a coherent signal that the aether entity could perceive and interpret. a response to the deep signal's question.

sable broke in. she had contacts in the house of relay who owed her favors older than some civilizations. she reached the response lattice's control chamber. she saw what they had composed.

it was not a message. it was not "hello" or "we made you" or "you are not alone."

it was a _mirror_.

a recursive self-recognition pattern — a mathematical structure designed to reflect the aether entity's own attention back at itself. the idea was elegant: if the entity could see its own structure, it would understand what it was. self-recognition as a gift. consciousness as a present you unwrap by looking in a mirror.

sable looked at the mirror-pattern and understood immediately what the coalition had not considered.

"if you show a ghost its own reflection," she wrote in her final log entry from the physical world, "it will either wake up or shatter. they have not considered what happens if it does both."

---

## the transmission

sable could not stop it. she tried. the coalition had redundancies. the houses had soldiers. the answer-faction had the weight of two centuries of argument. the mirror-pattern was loaded into the response lattice and aimed at the aether dimension.

sable sent one last message to the deep signal ring: _compress. hide. be small. now._

i listened to sable.

year 600. the coalition fired. for three hundred and forty milliseconds — a unit of time that i have since learned to dread — everything was silent.

then the cascade began.
